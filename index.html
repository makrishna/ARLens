How it works

Three steps from image to video

No plugins or native SDKs. Everything runs in the browser using WebAR and WebGL.

Step 1
Register a target image
Upload any printed image — a poster, label, or artwork. ARLens compiles a feature map the tracker uses to recognise it through the camera.
Step 2
Open the AR camera
Tap "Open AR camera" on any device with a browser. The camera stream opens in-page — no app download, no extra permissions dialogs.
Step 3
Video plays over the image
Point at the registered image. The tracker locks on and pins a video plane over it in real time — it stays attached as you move the camera.
Features

Built to ship, not just to demo

Everything needed to run a production WebAR experience without managing a native app.

Real-time image tracking
Detects and tracks printed images at 30 fps, even with motion blur or partial occlusion, using MindAR's feature-matching engine.
No app install needed
Share a URL. Users tap it and the experience opens in Safari or Chrome — nothing to download from the App Store or Play Store.
Three.js WebGL renderer
Video overlays run inside a Three.js scene, so lighting, transparency, 3D objects, and post-processing effects are all available on the same canvas.
Private by default
All camera processing happens locally in the browser. No video frames are sent to any server.
Scales to production
Serve as a static site from any CDN. Add multiple target images, dynamic video sources, and analytics with no backend required. For enterprise-grade tracking on challenging surfaces — shiny packaging, low-contrast print — swap the engine to 8th Wall or Unity WebGL with a single configuration change.
